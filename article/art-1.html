Why bacterial disease are not cured by Antibiotics?


What is Antibiotics?


Antibiotics are antimicrobial substance that are used against the bacteria.You have used it almost once in your lifetime.It becomes important part of human life especially those who live in urban area where sanitation is very less.


What is Antibiotic Resistance?

According to WHO, Antibiotic resistance occurs when bacteria change in response to the use of these medicines. Bacteria to which the antibiotic drug is administered develop resistance against that bacteria.

History of Antibiotic Resistance:-

It all started with the discovery of Penicillin by Alexander Fleming in 1928.It is widely used to treat infections of World War -II veterans.After decades of discovery of Penicillin,penicillin resistant strains of bacteria are discover.Then, new Beta-lactum antibiotics(it contains Beta lactum rings in their structure) are discovered.However,methicillin resistant Staphylococcus aureus is developed.Later, antibiotic Vancomycin was introduced in 1972 to counteract methicillin resistand bacteria. In 1979, Vancomycin resistant coagulase-negative staphylococci was identified.

Causes for Antibiotic Resistance:-

  • Overuse of Antibiotics by people
  • Inappropriate prescribing by some physician
  • Overuse of Antibiotics in livestock
  • Decrease in the discovery of Antibiotics

Effects Of Antibiotics Resistance:-

As you may now guessed,Antibiotic Resistance Decrease our ability to cure common infectious disease.A growing list of infections – such as pneumonia, tuberculosis, blood poisoning, gonorrhoea, and foodborne diseases – are becoming harder, and sometimes impossible, to treat as antibiotics become less effective.

Conclusion

As quoted by WHO Without urgent action, we are heading for a post-antibiotic era, in which common infections and minor injuries can once again kill. As topic states,it becomes more and more hard to treat a bacterial diseases.
